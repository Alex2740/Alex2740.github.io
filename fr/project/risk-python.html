À propos de Risk Python

Risk Python was a project I did during my prepa at Lycée Pierre Corneille. It was a research project on the theme of oceans. Some friends were working on the naval battle. I made me think about this project. I wondered if I could do it with Risk game. I noticed that there are different maps. It depends on the partnerships that Hasbro did. I wondered if the map and more specifically, the connections between territories influenced the winning strategy. I decided to create an AI which can play Risk to answer my question.

Probabilistic study

First, I started to study the probability of winning a battle (when a player sends his troops on an enemy territory to capture it). I started by writing a recursive function that calculated the probability, unfortunately this function was too slow. As soon as there were more than 5 troops, the calculation took more than an hour. I discovered dynamic programming, and modified my function to calculate using this approach. Like this my computer took about a tens of seconds to calculate a probability with more than a hundred troops. I was able to obtain the graph below showing the probability of winning a battle according to the number of attacking and defending troops.

Risk game modeling and AI creation

The Risk game was modeled using python object programming. There was one entity for the game, one for the board and one for the players. Then I created the AI that controlled a player. I put a parameter to the AI that controlled its degree of aggressiveness. A degree 1 corresponds to a player who attacks each time he could. A degree 0 corresponds to a player who never attacks and just defends. I also added a negative degree for a player who plays randomly (the witness).

Results

To study the winning strategy, I chose four different maps: the Lord of the Rings map, the classic map, the classic map with frozen oceans and the map from The Walking Dead.

The Lord of the Rings map has few connections and is almost linear while The Walking Dead map has many connections between territories.

The AI then played 200 games on each map with different degrees of aggressiveness. The following results were obtained.

We observe that the aggressive strategy is winning on a map with few connections and that on the contrary the defensive strategy is winning on a map with many connections.

This project allowed me to discover the world of artificial intelligence and programming. It was my first computer project.
